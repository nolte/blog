This blog is two things at once: a public log of the software projects I build,
and a personal knowledge base that I can search later. Every entry exists in
English and German.

## How posts are written

I don't sit down and write articles from scratch. Posts are drafted by Claude
from a short briefing — a topic, a few source files, sometimes a transcript of
a debugging session — and then I curate the result. That covers tone, accuracy,
and the small judgements that matter when you describe your own work. The
"AI" badge at the top of each post makes the workflow transparent.

The English version is always written first. The German translation follows
in the same pull request, so the two never drift apart.

## How it's organised

- **Blog** — posts, sorted newest first.
- **Projects** — a curated list of the software projects I work on publicly.
  Posts that grow out of a specific project link back here.
- **About** — the short version of what you're reading right now.

## What's next

Over time, posts will grow out of my [portfolio
projects](/projects): release notes worth elaborating on, architecture
decisions that took a while to land, lessons learned that I'd rather not
relearn next year.

If you want to follow along, the [RSS feed](/rss.xml) is the way.
